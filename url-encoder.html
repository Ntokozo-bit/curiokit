URL Encoder / Decoder

Make links safe for emails, chats, forms, and code — or decode them to read easily.

Why encode URLs?

  • Emails and chats sometimes break links with spaces or special characters.
  • Forms and APIs often require encoded values.
  • Decoding helps you read and debug long tracking links.

Need text counts or formatting help? Try the Word Counter or the Case Converter.

URL Encoder FAQ

Which characters get encoded?

Spaces, quotes, and non-ASCII characters are common candidates. Example: hello world → hello%20world.

Does it handle Unicode?

Yes, the built-in functions encode/decode Unicode safely.

Will this change my link’s meaning?

No — encoding preserves the original meaning, it just makes the link safe to transport.
